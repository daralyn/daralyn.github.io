2018 Trip to Kennedy Space Center (in Orlando, FL)

What we did/Why were we there?

In my junior year, I participated in a program called Student Spaceflight Experiments Program (SSEP). SSEP is partnered with NASA and gives students grades 6-12 opportunities to propose experiments that can be done in microgravity. The proposals are first submitted to the students' school, where the top three are picked. These three proposals get sent city wide, where one is chosen to be sent to the SSEP board. The SSEP board then selects experiments to be conducted on the ISS. My team's experiment was one of the forty-or-so experiments selected for flight in 2018.
We worked through many challenges to set up our experiment and send it to get packaged and launched to the ISS. The students and their families who sent their experiments to go into the ISS were invited to watch the launch of the rocket that would take our experiments into space. We were also given free admission into the Kennedy Space Center for one day, where we presented our experiments in the Atlantis building.
Here is an short article written by our school about our project: "Far Out Science"
