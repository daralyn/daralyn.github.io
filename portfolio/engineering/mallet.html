Mallet Overview

The purpose of this project was to make sure we know how to use certain machines. Once we finished this project, we are allowed to use certain machines in the shop that were used to make this project.

Explanation of the project

Our teacher used the waterjet to cut out the handle part. We were then given a plank of wood, which we had to use a saw to cut two blocks off of it to the right size. The two blocks were then put into the lazer cutter, and we lazer engraved one side and added a design on the other side. After that, we glued the two block onto either side of the handle to form the head of the mallet. After the glue dried, we used the drill press to drill the hole in the bottom of the mallet handle so you can hang it up. We also drilled two holes in the mallet head to fit dowels to keep the blocks in line when the mallet is used. We cut the dowels using the band saw, then glued the dowels into the mallethead, and used the Japanese saw to cut off the big chuncks of dowels, a dremel for the chunchs too small to use the Japanese saw, but too small to use sand paper, and sand paper to sand it down to the surface. We used a rasp to smooth out the sharp edges on the mallet handle, and the belt and disc sanders to sand off the extra material on the mallet head.
