Flat Pack Project

March-April 2018

Quick Overview

In this project, we created a 3D object that could be pressed together with multiple 2D pieces. The constraints were that the piece should fit on a 2'x5' board of plywood, and could use either 1/2" or 1/4" thickness. We again used the Maverick Router. My pieces were cut on the 1/4" thick plywood with a 1/8" end mill.

Reflections

What I learned...

I learned more about using Fusion's parameters/variables. Because I had to cut my pieces many times, I also learned more about the pros and cons of using a vacuum table, and also using challenges of using clamps/screws to secure the workpiece down with.
The first time I cut it, there were some tabs that were missing because though the sketch was correct, when I extruded the pieces, some parts didn't come up. Other difficulties were figuring out how long some of the pieces had to be in order for them to fit. It took me four or five more times to get it right. It was a really great learning experience and shows how easy it is to make mistakes.

Challenges...

This project was really challenging to get everything right. I had a lot of pieces, and Fusion 360 was really slow on my laptop, so sometimes I forgot what I needed to fix, or if I had already fixed it. I learned that a dedicated graphics card could help with that. One of the challenges was that after my pieces were cut out, seeing what pieces fit with each other, and how the other pieces needed to be changed in order to fit better.

If I were to do it again...

One thing I would change would be to have made a prototype of it first, maybe out of a different material, or scale it down somehow, just to see if all the pieces would fit together. I think I would have also made a few different sketches, instead of having one sketch for the entire sheet of plywood. Maybe that would have made it faster.
